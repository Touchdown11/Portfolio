Lecture Notes & Summary

Key principles and concepts from CV Writing Lecture

Purpose of a CV

A CV is a comprehensive document that summarizes your qualifications, skills, and experiences to make a strong first impression and secure job interviews.

Key Principles

  • First Impression: Well-structured, visually appealing, and error-free
  • Tailoring: Customize for each specific job application
  • Research: Understand the company and use keywords from job descriptions

CV Structure & Formatting

Clear layout with consistent formatting, proper use of headings, bullet points, and white space for easy readability.

Essential Components

  • Personal Information
  • Professional Summary
  • Work Experience
  • Education
  • Skills
  • Achievements & Awards

CV Checklist

Contact information is complete and professional
CV is tailored to the specific job application
Clear, professional summary at the top
Work experience listed in reverse chronological order
Quantifiable achievements included
Skills section is relevant and well-organized
Education section is complete and accurate
Formatting is consistent and professional
No spelling or grammatical errors

Essential CV Components

Key elements that make up an effective CV

01

Personal Information

  • Full name and contact details
  • Professional email and phone number
  • LinkedIn profile (if relevant)
  • Location (city, country)
02

Professional Summary

  • Concise career overview
  • Key skills and qualifications
  • Tailored to specific job applications
  • Highlights what you bring to the role
03

Work Experience

  • Reverse chronological order
  • Job title, company, dates
  • Responsibilities and achievements
  • Action verbs and quantifiable results
04

Education

  • Degrees and certifications
  • University/college names
  • Graduation dates
  • Relevant coursework or honors
05

Skills

  • Hard and soft skills
  • Categorized by type
  • Relevant to target position
  • Honest proficiency levels
06

Achievements & Awards

  • Professional recognitions
  • Certifications and licenses
  • Relevant accomplishments
  • Quantifiable successes

This lecture was extremely practical and shifted my perspective on a CV from being a simple life summary to a strategic marketing document. I learned that its primary purpose is not to list everything I've ever done, but to strategically present the most relevant information to a specific employer to solve their needs.

The connection to ATS software was a revelation. Understanding that a machine might screen my CV before a human even sees it makes the use of specific keywords not just a suggestion, but a necessity. ATS Optimization Tips

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) are used by many companies to screen CVs. Here's how to optimize for them:

Keywords and Phrases

Identify relevant keywords from the job description and incorporate them naturally throughout your CV. This increases the chances of your CV being selected by the ATS.

  • Use specific job titles and skills mentioned in the posting
  • Include industry-specific terminology
  • Mirror the language used in the job description

Formatting for ATS

Some formatting choices can interfere with how ATS systems read your CV. Follow these guidelines:

  • Use standard section headings (e.g., "Work Experience", "Education")
  • Avoid tables, columns, and graphics that might confuse the system
  • Save your CV as a .docx or .pdf file (check the employer's preference)
  • Use a clean, simple font like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman

Key CV Writing Tips

Practical advice for creating an effective CV

Research Before Writing

Thoroughly research the company and position to understand their needs, culture, and keywords to incorporate into your CV.

Use Action Verbs

Begin bullet points with strong action verbs (e.g., "managed," "developed," "implemented") to create dynamic descriptions.

Quantify Achievements

Wherever possible, include numbers, percentages, or other metrics to demonstrate the scale and impact of your accomplishments.

Proofread Meticulously

Eliminate all spelling and grammatical errors, and ensure consistent formatting throughout the document for a professional appearance.

Keep It Concise

Aim for a clear, focused CV that highlights your most relevant qualifications, typically no more than two pages for most professionals.
