Dining Basics

Fundamental principles of professional dining etiquette

Before the Meal

  • Take your seat once you get a cue from the host
  • Silence your phone before sitting down
  • Place your napkin on your lap after everyone has been seated
  • Fold your napkin in half with the crease toward you
  • Wait for the host to begin eating before you start

During the Meal

  • Keep elbows off the table
  • Chew with your mouth closed
  • Don't talk with your mouth full
  • Keep your unused hand in your lap
  • Pace your eating with others at the table
  • Make light conversation with everyone

After the Meal

  • Lay your fork and knife at the 4:00/10:00 position
  • Leave plate where it is - don't push it away
  • Used napkin goes next to your plate
  • Thank the host before leaving
  • Do not ask for a doggy bag at formal events

Utensil Placement

  • Forks go on the left, knives and spoons on the right
  • Utensils are used from the outside in
  • Dessert utensils are placed above the plate or brought with dessert
  • Butter knife is placed on the bread plate

Glassware Arrangement

  • Water glass is placed above the dinner knife
  • Red wine glass is next to water glass
  • White wine glass is placed to the right of red wine glass
  • Champagne flute may be added for toasts

Essential Etiquette Rules

Key guidelines for impeccable dining behavior

Napkin Etiquette

  • Place napkin on lap after everyone is seated
  • Use your napkin as necessary throughout the meal
  • If leaving temporarily, place napkin on your chair
  • At meal's end, place napkin loosely to the left of your plate
  • Don't use your napkin to clean spills - ask server for help

Utensil Usage

  • Hold utensils properly - not like a pencil
  • American style: Switch fork to right hand after cutting
  • Continental style: Keep fork in left hand
  • Rest utensils on plate when pausing during meal
  • Place utensils parallel on plate when finished

Special Foods

  • Bread: Break into small pieces, butter each piece individually
  • Soup: Spoon away from you, sip from the side of the spoon
  • Pasta: Twirl a few strands against the plate with fork
  • French fries: Use fingers if served with burgers/sandwiches
  • Bacon: Use fingers only if very crisp

Cocktail Party Etiquette

Navigating social business events with confidence

Conversation Skills

Safe topics: Sports, books, movies, art, travel. Avoid controversial subjects like politics.

Timing & Movement

Stay no more than 10 minutes in one conversation. Circulate to meet different people.

Food & Drink Handling

Hold drink in left hand to keep right hand free for handshakes. Know your alcohol limit.

Arrival Etiquette

Whoever arrives at the door first should open it for others. Defer to senior executives.

Cocktail Party Tips

Conversation Starters

Ask open-ended questions to encourage dialogue. Listen actively and show genuine interest. Be more interested than interesting.

Making Connections

Introduce yourself with your first name. Maintain eye contact during handshakes. Have business cards ready to exchange.

Body Language

Stand with confident posture. Avoid fidgeting. Use open gestures. Smile genuinely. Respect personal space.

Special Food Handling

Proper techniques for handling different types of foods

Bread & Pastries

Bread Rolls: Break into small pieces with your fingers, butter each piece individually.
Pastries: Cut in halves or quarters and eat with fingers or fork.
French Fries: Eat with fingers if served with sandwiches or burgers.

Meat & Seafood

Steak: Cut one piece at a time, eat with fork.
Fish: Use fish knife and fork, remove bones discreetly.
Bacon: Only very crisp bacon may be eaten with fingers.

Other Foods

Pasta: Separate a few strands with fork. Twirl onto fork with tines held against the plate.
Potatoes: Eat baked potatoes with a fork. Skins with knife and fork.
Lemon Wedge: Squeeze over fish with fingers, use fork to catch seeds.
