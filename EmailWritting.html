Lecture Notes & Summary

Key principles and concepts from the Email Writing lecture

Purpose of Professional Emails

Professional emails serve as formal communication tools to convey information, make requests, and build professional relationships in academic and business settings.

Key Principles

  • Clarity: Clear subject lines and concise content
  • Professionalism: Appropriate tone and language
  • Structure: Proper formatting and organization
  • Etiquette: Respectful communication practices

Email Structure & Formatting

Clear layout with proper salutations, organized content, professional closing, and signature for effective communication.

Essential Components

  • Professional Subject Line
  • Appropriate Salutation
  • Clear and Concise Body
  • Professional Closing
  • Email Signature

Essential Email Components

Key elements that make up an effective professional email

01

Subject Line

  • Clear and specific about email content
  • Avoids greetings like "Hello" or "Greetings"
  • Uses logical keywords for searchability
  • Includes name only in job applications
02

Salutation

  • Formal greetings ("Dear + Title + Last Name")
  • Appropriate for the relationship with recipient
  • Avoids informal greetings
  • Uses correct titles (Professor, Dr., Mr./Ms.)
03

Email Body

  • Clear and concise content
  • Professional tone and language
  • Organized paragraphs with clear purpose
  • Appropriate level of detail
04

Closing

  • Professional sign-offs (Best, Kind regards)
  • Avoids informal closings (Love, Yours)
  • Includes full name
  • Matches tone of the email
05

Email Signature

  • Full name and contact information
  • Professional title or program
  • University or company affiliation
  • Relevant professional links
06

Response Etiquette

  • Timely responses (within 24-48 hours)
  • Appropriate use of "Reply" vs. "Reply All"
  • Answers all questions from previous email
  • Maintains professional tone in follow-ups

Tone Transformation Exercise

Transforming informal or demanding language into professional communication:

Before

"I need to cancel my appointment tomorrow. Let me know when I can reschedule."

After

"I need to cancel my appointment scheduled for tomorrow. Would it be possible to reschedule for sometime next week? Please let me know what availability you have."

Subject Line Practice

Scenario: You need to request an extension for a project deadline from your professor.

Poor Subject Lines:

Help needed Urgent Project problem

Improved Subject Lines:

Request for project extension - [Course Name] Extension request for [Project Name] Question about project deadline - [Your Name]

Email Etiquette Guidelines

Key principles for maintaining professionalism in email communication:

Response Time Expectations

Understanding appropriate response times for different types of emails:

  • Urgent matters: Respond within a few hours if possible
  • Standard professional emails: Respond within 24-48 hours
  • Weekends and holidays: Response expected by next business day
  • After-hours emails: Don't expect immediate responses

Reply vs. Reply All

Knowing when to use "Reply" versus "Reply All":

  • Reply: Use when your response is only relevant to the sender
  • Reply All: Use when your response is relevant to all recipients
  • Consideration: Avoid cluttering inboxes with unnecessary "Reply All" responses
  • Exception: When specifically asked to respond to all

Key Email Writing Tips

Practical advice for creating effective professional emails

Craft Clear Subject Lines

Your subject line should accurately reflect the email's content and purpose. Avoid vague subjects that might be overlooked or misunderstood.

Use Appropriate Salutations

Address recipients with proper titles and last names unless you have an established informal relationship. When in doubt, err on the side of formality.

Keep It Concise

Respect the recipient's time by being clear and to the point. Use paragraphs to organize your thoughts and make the email easier to read.

Proofread Carefully

Always review your email for spelling, grammar, and clarity before sending. Errors can undermine your professionalism and the message's impact.

Include a Professional Signature

Create a consistent email signature with your contact information. This adds credibility and makes it easy for recipients to respond or contact you through other channels.

Email Template Application

Implementing the principles learned in practical email formats

Absence Notification Template

Subject: Absence from [Course Name] on [Date]

Dear Professor [Last Name],

I am writing to inform you that I was unable to attend your [Course Name] class on [Date] due to [brief reason - e.g., illness]. I have already connected with classmates to review the material covered, but I would appreciate any additional resources or guidance you could provide.

Please let me know if there are any specific assignments I should prioritize to catch up.

Thank you for your understanding.

Sincerely,
[Your Full Name]

Appointment Request Template

Subject: Appointment request to discuss [Topic]

Dear Dr. [Last Name],

After reviewing the lecture materials on [Topic] from [Date], I still have some questions about [specific aspect]. Would it be possible to schedule a brief meeting sometime this week to discuss this further?

I am available on [Day] between [Time] and [Time], or on [Alternative Day] between [Time] and [Time]. Please let me know if either of these times works for you, or if you have another availability that might be better.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Best regards,
[Your Full Name]
