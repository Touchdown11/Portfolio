About Our Project

Discover the story behind our successful food stall at the university festival

As part of our Professional Skills module, we participated in the Food Festival by managing our stall, Chandarapala. The main objective of this event was to enhance our teamwork, communication, problem-solving, and entrepreneurial skills while offering attendees a memorable food experience.

Our stall featured a diverse menu that included both savory and sweet options, carefully chosen to appeal to a wide range of customers. The experience allowed our group to practice and strengthen skills such as time management, financial planning, coordination, marketing, and customer service.

Overall, managing the stall gave us not only the opportunity to engage directly with customers but also valuable hands-on experience in running a business-like setup. It was a successful and rewarding learning experience for all group members.

Challenges & Lessons Learned

Reflections on our journey and insights for future projects

Challenges Faced

  • Workspace Limitations: Limited space and equipment made food preparation tiring.
  • Financial Loss: Incurred a loss due to preparing too many food items with large investment.
  • Shortage of Supplies: Ran out of ice midway, requiring last-minute purchase.
  • Time Management: Preparing many varieties took more time than expected.
  • Selling Difficulties: Some items didn't sell well due to low customer interest and competition.

Lessons Learned

  • Limit the menu to high-demand items and practice careful budgeting.
  • Better planning, smaller menu, and clearer task allocation save time and improve productivity.
  • Successful sales require strong marketing, attractive presentation, and customer engagement.
  • Choose items that match customer preferences to maximize sales.
  • Teamwork and communication are crucial for overcoming unexpected challenges.

Recommendations for Future

  • Allocate more time for food preparation to avoid last-minute shortages.
  • Ensure sufficient change is available before the event.
  • Finalize and organize decoration plans several days in advance.
  • Create a clear crowd management plan.
  • Establish a detailed task division plan for the event day.
