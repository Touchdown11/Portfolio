What Separates Successful Candidates

Key differences between candidates who succeed and those who don't

Successful Candidates

  • Were specific and consistent about position
  • Used the company name frequently
  • Showed research about the company
  • Responded positively to information
  • Made eye contact often and smiled
  • Accepted topic shifts gracefully
  • Used appropriate technical jargon
  • Asked thoughtful questions
  • Answered with specifics and supported claims
  • Spoke more than interviewer(s)

Unsuccessful Candidates

  • Had vague ideas about the position
  • Rarely used the company name
  • Didn't know about the company
  • Responded in neutral tone
  • Made little eye contact
  • Resisted shift of topic
  • Avoided using industry jargon
  • Asked few or no questions
  • Gave short, unsupported answers
  • Spoke less than interviewer(s)

STAR Method Practice

Transforming generic answers into structured responses using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result):

Before

"I'm good at working in teams. I collaborated on a university project."

After

"In my university food festival project (Situation), our team needed to coordinate multiple tasks under a tight deadline (Task). I took initiative to create a shared timeline and organized daily check-ins to ensure alignment (Action). As a result, we completed the project ahead of schedule and received positive feedback from our instructor (Result)."

Interview Preparation Strategies

Effective techniques to prepare for different types of interview questions:

Behavioral Questions

Prepare stories using the STAR method that demonstrate key competencies like leadership, problem-solving, and teamwork.

  • Identify 5-7 key stories from your experience
  • Practice delivering them concisely (2-3 minutes each)
  • Focus on your specific role and contributions
  • Highlight quantifiable results where possible

Technical/Skill-Based Questions

Review the job description thoroughly and identify the key skills required.

  • Brush up on technical knowledge relevant to the role
  • Prepare examples that demonstrate your proficiency
  • Be honest about your skill level while showing willingness to learn
  • Practice explaining complex concepts in simple terms

Interview Structure & Answer Framework

Organizing your responses for maximum impact

1

Introduction

Make a strong first impression with confident greeting and positive body language

2

Evidence-Speaking

Provide specific examples and stories that demonstrate your qualifications

3

Your Questions

Ask thoughtful questions that show your interest and research

4

Closure

End positively, reiterate interest, and thank the interviewer

Sample Question: "Tell us about a challenge you've faced and how you handled it"
Signpost: "OK, I'm going to tell you about the time when I..."
Context: "My goal was as follows... to achieve this objective I did three things..."
Result: "As a result of my actions..."
Complete Answer Example:

"In my university project, we were facing a tight deadline for our food festival project. My team members had conflicting schedules, and we were falling behind. I took the initiative to reorganize our workflow, delegated tasks according to each member's strengths, and created a shared timeline. I also scheduled daily check-ins to monitor progress. As a result, we not only met the deadline but received positive feedback for our thorough planning and execution. This experience taught me valuable project management and team coordination skills."
